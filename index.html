Who are honeybees?

Honeybees (genus Apis) are social insects that live in colonies. A typical colony contains a single queen, many workers (sterile females), and seasonally some drones (males).

Anatomy & roles

  • Queen: primary egg-layer and pheromone source that helps regulate colony behavior.
  • Workers: perform foraging, nursing, comb building, defense, and hive maintenance across their short lives.
  • Drones: mate with queens; they do not forage or work in the hive.

Threats & How to Help Honeybees

Author: Honeybee Notes ¬∑ Updated Jan 1, 2026

Main threats

  • Pesticides: Certain insecticides can kill bees directly or impair navigation and immune responses.
  • Habitat loss: Loss of wildflower meadows and nesting sites reduces forage and nesting choices.
  • Parasites & disease: Varroa mites, viruses, and fungal infections can weaken colonies.
  • Climate & weather extremes: affect floral resources and colony resilience.

How you can help

  1. Plant pollinator-friendly flowers that bloom across seasons.
  2. Avoid or reduce pesticide use; choose bee-safe alternatives.
  3. Provide water and sheltered spaces (logs, insect hotels).
  4. Support local beekeepers and conservation groups.
